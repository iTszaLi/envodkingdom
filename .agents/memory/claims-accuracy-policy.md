# Claims-accuracy policy for ENVOD marketing copy

Any copy edit (EN and AR together — this is a bilingual site, never edit one language alone) must obey these accuracy rules. They come from a client mandate to keep claims legally defensible.

## Banned marketing claims (soften everywhere)
- No "guaranteed" / "we guarantee" as a service promise (e.g. "24-hour clearance guarantee", "guaranteed transit times").
- No fabricated coverage numbers like "87 countries" or ranking claims like "top 10".
- No absolute superlatives about ENVOD or "in the Kingdom/KSA" — e.g. "fastest in the Kingdom", "the fastest, most reliable clearance". Comparatives like "one of the fastest and simplest ways" are acceptable.
- Flat "24-hour"/"in 24 hours" outcome claims (headlines, SEO titles/descriptions, bullets) must be hedged to **"typically within 24 hours"** / **"often within 24 hours"** (AR: "عادةً خلال 24 ساعة" / "غالباً خلال 24 ساعة"). This includes the top-bar, hero chapters, section H2s, service tags, and pillar SEO meta.

## Legitimately factual — KEEP (do NOT soften)
- The ATA Carnet's financial **duty guarantee** ("guarantees the duties to customs", "guarantee arranged") — that is how the carnet instrument works, not a marketing promise.
- "Air freight is the **fastest mode** of international shipping/transport" — an industry fact about the transport mode, not a company/Kingdom superlative.
- "largest / biggest exhibitions" — describes the events (LEAP, Big 5, etc.), not ENVOD.
- Coverage figures intentionally kept: **ATA 80+ countries**, **Air 50+ countries**.

## Mandated EXACT hedged sentences (use verbatim when the topic comes up)
- Customs: "Customs clearance completed as quickly as possible, with many shipments typically cleared within 24 hours depending on shipment type, documentation, customs requirements, and regulatory approvals."
- Transit: "Typical transit times may vary depending on origin, destination, carrier schedules, customs procedures, and seasonal factors."

**Why:** client compliance requirement — unhedged superlatives/guarantees are a legal exposure.
**How to apply:** before adding or editing any homepage/service/pillar marketing string, grep the site for the banned terms (English AND Arabic: "الأسرع", "مضمونة/نضمن", "ضمان") and confirm any survivor is one of the factual exceptions above.
