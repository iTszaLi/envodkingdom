Redesign the "End-to-End Exhibition Logistics Workflow" section to look like a premium enterprise logistics process similar to DHL, Maersk, DP World, DSV, and Kuehne+Nagel. The current horizontal timeline with numbered circles feels too basic and does not communicate the value of our services.

Overall Design

Replace the current timeline with a modern, professional workflow consisting of 7 premium process cards connected by a thin horizontal line or subtle directional arrows.

The section should feel clean, structured, and informative—not overly animated.

Layout

Create seven equally sized cards arranged horizontally on desktop.

On tablets and mobile, stack them into two columns or a vertical timeline.

Each card should contain:

A clean professional icon
Step number (01–07)
Step title
A short 1–2 sentence description
Optional estimated timeframe (where appropriate)

Example:

01 Consultation

📞 Icon

Understand your exhibition requirements, shipment size, venue, deadlines, and special handling requirements.

02 Planning

📋 Icon

Prepare the logistics plan, transportation schedule, customs documentation, and delivery timeline.

03 Documentation

📄 Icon

Manage ATA Carnet, customs clearance, permits, and all required shipping documents.

04 Transportation

🚛 Icon

Transport cargo safely via air, sea, or road while providing shipment tracking.

05 Venue Delivery

🏢 Icon

Deliver directly to the exhibition venue according to organizer schedules and booth setup timelines.

06 On-site Support

👷 Icon

Coordinate unloading, handling, temporary storage, and provide on-site logistics assistance throughout the exhibition.

07 Return Logistics

📦 Icon

Arrange packing, customs processing, and return shipping after the exhibition concludes.

Visual Style

Each card should have:

Dark premium background
16–20px rounded corners
Thin border
Soft shadow
High-quality outline icon
Large bold step number
Clean typography
Plenty of white space

Cards should feel similar to enterprise SaaS dashboards.

Connection Style

Instead of a heavy timeline:

Connect the cards using a thin subtle line.
Place a small arrow between each card.
Keep the connection understated.
Remove the oversized circles from the current design.
Animations

Keep animations minimal and professional.

Fade-up animation when the section enters the viewport.
Slight card elevation (4–6px) on hover.
Soft border highlight using the brand color.
Icon gently scales by 5%.
No bouncing, spinning, or flashy animations.
No continuous movement.
Add Trust Indicators

At the bottom of the workflow, include a small trust strip:

✓ ATA Carnet Specialists

✓ Customs Clearance Experts

✓ On-Time Venue Delivery

✓ 24/7 Shipment Tracking

✓ Dedicated Project Manager

These should appear as elegant badges or chips.

Optional Enhancement

At the end of the workflow, after Step 07, add a subtle call-to-action:

Need exhibition logistics support?

Get a Free Logistics Consultation →

This should blend naturally into the design without looking like a large advertisement.

Performance
Use SVG icons.
Keep animations GPU-accelerated.
Ensure excellent responsiveness.
Maintain fast loading and good Core Web Vitals.
Overall Goal

The section should clearly explain our complete exhibition logistics process while reinforcing professionalism, reliability, and expertise. It should look like a workflow presented by a global logistics company—not a generic timeline. The design should immediately build trust with corporate clients and make the process easy to understand at a glance.
